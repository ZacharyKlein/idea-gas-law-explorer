How the Laws Fit Together

Boyle's Law
PV = k

For fixed n and T, pressure and volume move in opposite directions.

Charles' Law
V/T = k

For fixed P and n, volume follows absolute temperature.

Ideal Gas Law
PV = nRT

One equation connects all four state variables using the gas constant R.

Gay-Lussac's Law
P/T = k

For fixed V and n, pressure follows absolute temperature.

Avogadro's Law
V/n = k

For fixed P and T, volume follows the number of moles.

Derivation

From Simple Laws to PV = nRT

1

Boyle: V ∝ 1/P when n and T are constant.

2

Charles: V ∝ T when P and n are constant.

3

Avogadro: V ∝ n when P and T are constant.

4

Combine the proportionalities: V ∝ nT/P.

5

Multiply by pressure: PV ∝ nT.

6

Replace proportionality with the gas constant R: PV = nRT.
